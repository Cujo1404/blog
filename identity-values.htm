Core personal values

  • Using the evidence gathered, describe your core personal values.
    - Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took.
    In my first year at Yoobee, I was 20 years old, one of the last projects was a group project. No one else in my team put their hands up to be the leader, so I did. A week into this 3-4 week project I realised my mental health was deteriorating and I wasn't able to handle the workload required of me. I wasn't being a good leader. At that point I explained my situation to my tutor and discussed who else in my team may be the best fit, this took a lot for me to admit I was struggling, I don't usually talk about these kinds of things. We decided on someone and they were gracefully open to taking on the leadership role. The rest of the project went so smoothly, and the end product was better than anyone expected. But I can't stop thinking about how much of my pride I had to swallow that day, to admit I wasn't a good fit, to admit I was wrong, to admit that I knew someone could do the job way better than I could. It was a true moment of self-realisation.

    - Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.
    2021, the Yoobee Prelude graduation project, also a group project. We crafted a team of individuals with whom we're technically capable but still worked well in a team. some of us were more design-focused, others code-focused. 3 of us worked on the design, 2 more joined a couple of days later when that part was over. I felt there was quite a lot of push back between designers and developers, each of us wanted the website built 'our way'. there was one particular moment where we just couldn't decide on a way to display the student portfolio. I mediated a discussion on the outcomes of each option, let each person share why they think it's best, in the end, I was able to suggest an option that satisfied both people and we went on our merry way. looking back I feel that was a defining moment where I used my 'big picture thinking', it felt good to settle what could have been a big argument.
